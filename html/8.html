AT last! Australians are finally refusing to be treated like scum, too stupid and vicious to be free to speak.

True, I might be kidding myself. Looking for dawn in deepest night. These are terrible days for free speech, after all.

BLOG WITH ANDREW BOLT

How could we have let ourselves be so muzzled? How could we have so lost our pride and our freedom?

Consider: right now we have three Queensland University of Technology students being sued by an Aboriginal staffer for racism — though they complained against racism.

Yes, their crime was to write social media messages protesting at being kicked off computers that the university insisted were only for Aboriginal students. This was “segregation”, they said.

And they’re right. So we now have laws — the Racial Discrimination Act — that silence students for protesting against discrimination.

Are we crazy?

And who would have dreamt that a Liberal government would be too gutless to change these wicked laws for fear of seeming “racist” too?

Did you know that Attorney-General George Brandis even refused to indemnify these students to spare them horrendous legal bills?

But in many other ways, we have let ourselves be stopped from saying what we think or reading what we’d like. We have let others decide for us what is fit to say and believe.

For instance, last year we let a rat-pack of student activists and academic unionists stop two Australian universities from hiring a world-renowned academic, Professor Bjorn Lomborg.

Lomborg’s sin? To correctly note that global-warming schemes wasted billions of dollars without doing much good.

Ban him, howled the National Tertiary Education Union, for the thought crime of “downgrading the importance to the world of climate change mitigation”.

Once again, this government — this Liberal government — surrendered. It gave in to this lynch mob and stopped the funding for Lomborg, rather than insist on free inquiry in our universities.

How many more examples do you want?

Just last week, Greens leader Richard Di Natale, with the backing of the state-funded ABC, asked the Australian Press Council to punish cartoonist Bill Leak for drawing a cartoon in The Australian which highlighted the shocking levels of child abuse in Aboriginal communities.

Worse, our Race Discrimination Commissioner, Tim Soutphommasane, actually urged Australians to complain to his Human Rights Commission about Leak, so it could take legal action.

How can we have a commission for human rights that actually hates free speech?

And, again, this so-called Liberal government wouldn’t defend either Leak or his freedom. In fact, two ministers (Ken Wyatt and Nigel Scullion) condemned him, and not one stuck up for him.

Nor would the Attorney-General pull Soutphommasane, a former Labor speechwriter, into line.

What is this? What has become of this country, once the land of the larrikin who’d speak fearlessly to anyone, high or low?

On it goes. This year, Tasmania’s Anti-Discrimination Commission had the hide to order Australia’s Catholic bishops to show cause why they shouldn’t be stopped from defending traditional marriage.

Meanwhile, the state-funded ABC and SBS — both meant to be impartial — scream abuse at conservatives, to intimidate them into silence.

“Dog-f---er”, one ABC show called conservative Chris Kenny. “Go f---yourself, Bill,” another SBS show jeered at Leak.

And, of course, the ABC, meant by law to be balanced in exchange for $1 billion a year of taxpayers’ money, has instead for years stopped conservatives from hosting any of its main current affairs shows.

Have we lost our brains or our guts? How could Australians have let themselves be shut up and shut down like this?

But I should declare an interest. Two of my own articles were banned by the Federal Court for being too upsetting to one.

Banned! Under that wicked Racial Discrimination Act again.

And now “diversity” officials in the ABC are actually trying to stifle diversity by demanding the ABC not screen a documentary it commissioned in which Aboriginal MP Linda Burney and I politely debate the government’s plan to change our Constitution to divide us by race. These officials, including the ABC’s Bonner committee of Aboriginal representatives, insist the ABC should not give me a platform to put the argument against this constitutional change. They claim I am a “racist” for opposing things like that. They demand, therefore, that I not be heard in case viewers are influenced or angered.

How can such arguments be seriously put in this country?

How can free speech be held in such contempt?

And that contempt is for you, too. Remember: the censors’ aim is not really to stop me speaking but to stop you from hearing.

You see, they believe you are so savage that you cannot be trusted to hear me without smashing stuff.

But stop. As I said, just when all seemed lost, Australians started to revolt. Everywhere, I now hear the cry: Enough!

In last month’s election, 600,000 Australians voted for Pauline Hanson’s One Nation in the Senate — many simply to protest against the bullying.

Another million people voted for other parties, including the Liberal Democrats, Family First and Derryn Hinch, who had also promised to scrap the Racial Discrimination Act.

That’s not all. On Friday, Tony Abbott said he was now sorry he caved in to pressure as prime minister and reneged on his promise to reform those laws.

He made it clear he wouldn’t make that mistake again if given the chance, protesting that “our parliament prefers to tolerate over-the-top prosecutions than to upset thin-skinned activists”.

Abbott won’t be without allies in wanting his rival, Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull, to change the race act, either.

On Saturday, the Liberals’ WA branch also called for the law to be changed so it was no longer unlawful to merely insult or offend someone with your opinion.

Many Liberal MPs — including impressive newcomers James Paterson and Andrew Hastie, and conservative warriors such as Cory Bernardi — agree.

And Turnbull faces serious instability if he keeps stonewalling.

Indeed, he could even lose his leadership, with the forces for change gathering strength.

Also on Friday, News Corp Australia, the country’s biggest newspaper group and publisher of this paper, made clear it had had enough of this creeping censorship, too.

Speaking at the group’s annual awards night, News Corp’s international boss, Robert Thomson, urged journalists, cartoonists and editors to fight political correctness and the enemies of free speech.

What’s more, Leak was named the group’s cartoonist of the year, and, Paul Whittaker, The Australian’s editor-in-chief, passionately defended him.

If the Press Council and the Human Rights Commission try to punish Leak, they will buy a war with News Corp and countless Australians who are now jack of our arrogant censors.

This assault by the Left on free speech has abused our intelligence and our freedom.

Australians, insulted and offended, are now saying they are as mad as hell and will not take this any more.

It’s time to fight.
